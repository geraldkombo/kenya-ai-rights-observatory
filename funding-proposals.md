## Proposal 1: CIPESA Africa Digital Rights Fund (ADRF)

**Format:** Concept Note (Email/Form Submission)
**Target Ask:** $10,000 (Seed funding for field validation)

**Subject:** ADRF Proposal: Localizing Digital Rights Monitoring via the Kenya Digital Rights Risk Atlas

**Project Title:** The Kenya Digital Rights Risk Atlas: Decentralizing Accountability
**Applicant:** [Project Lead Name] & Gerald Kombo (Technical Lead)

**1. Project Context & Problem Statement**
While digital rights violations in East Africa are well-documented at the national level, the localization of these risks remains opaque. In Kenya, the aggressive deployment of algorithmic welfare systems, biometric enrollment mandates, and localized network throttling disproportionately impacts marginalized and rural populations. National averages obscure the reality that data privacy risks and surveillance densities vary drastically across the 47 counties. Local civil society organizations lack accessible, empirical tools to challenge county-level tech procurement or advocate for vulnerable demographics.

**2. The Solution**
We have engineered the Kenya Digital Rights Risk Atlas — an offline-capable, interactive civic technology platform. The Atlas tracks a simple Digital Rights Risk Score (DRRS) across all 47 counties, quantifying Surveillance Density, Internet Health Deficits, Data Privacy Risks, Biometric Exclusion, and Platform Impact.

Built on a completely static Next.js architecture, the platform operates without tracking cookies, requires virtually no bandwidth to load (utilizing TopoJSON compression), and enables localized advocates to generate immediate "Share Cards" for offline dissemination via WhatsApp.

**3. Use of Funds**
The platform's technical scaffolding is entirely self-funded and currently operational. We are seeking $10,000 from the ADRF to execute Phase 2: Field Validation & Capacity Building.

- **Data Ground-Truthing ($4,000):** Transitioning proxy estimates for "Internet Health" and "Biometric Exclusion" to verified county-level metrics through FOIA requests and local enumerators.
- **Advocacy Training Workshops ($6,000):** Conducting two regional workshops to train community organizers and psychosocial support workers on utilizing the DRRS data to file localized complaints with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC).

**4. Impact**
By democratizing access to this data, we transition digital rights advocacy from Nairobi-centric policy discussions directly to the grassroots level, enabling targeted interventions where biometric tracking and digital exclusion intersect with human welfare.

## Proposal 2: Open Technology Fund (OTF) - Internet Freedom Fund

**Format:** Initial Concept Note
**Target Ask:** $48,500

**Project Name:** Kenya Digital Rights Observatory: Low-Bandwidth Infrastructure for Internet Freedom
**Primary Contact:** [Project Lead Name]

**Brief Description:**
A localized, open-source geospatial dashboard engineered for 2G/3G environments that monitors internet shutdowns, surveillance procurement, and biometric exclusion across Kenya's decentralized governance structure.

**How does this project address internet freedom?**
Authoritarian tech deployment is increasingly localized. The Observatory exposes these micro-level violations by visualizing network disruption hours alongside CCTV and biometric expansion at the county level. By explicitly correlating network shutdowns with regions experiencing high surveillance deployment or civic unrest, the tool provides real-time, undeniable evidence of coordinated digital repression.

Furthermore, the Observatory protects its users by design. It operates entirely client-side without servers, databases, or analytics tracking, ensuring that human rights defenders accessing the tool in highly monitored regions leave no digital footprint.

**What are the specific deliverables?**
1. **Open Data API & Export Pipeline:** Finalizing the automated extraction of DRRS metrics into sanitized, machine-readable CSVs and establishing an embeddable widget network for syndication across East African CSO websites.
2. **Longitudinal Tracking Engine:** Implementing a time-series architecture to track the YoY variance of shutdown hours and data privacy complaints from 2024 to 2026.
3. **Investigative Reporting Series:** Funding a 3-part data journalism series exposing the procurement pipelines of AI surveillance systems in peripheral counties.

**Target Audience:**
Grassroots human rights defenders, legal advocates litigating against exclusionary digital ID systems, and regional journalists who currently lack the technical infrastructure to synthesize complex network shutdown data into actionable local stories.
